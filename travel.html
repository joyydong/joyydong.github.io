Travel

Seattle, WA

June 1-August 24, 2019

I spent summer 2019 interning at Amazon in the Pacific Northwest. Though I was a bit worried about living in a new city on my own, I quickly adjusted to the Seattleite life. I worked at South Lake Union, so on weekdays after work, I would go for a run around the lake (gorgeous run, highly recommend). Weekends were spent exploring the city, hiking, watching sunsets, and partaking in tourist activities! I had a blast living in this beautiful city and interning on an amazing team at Amazon.

Houston, TX

September 26-28, 2018

I had the privilege of attending the 2018 Grace Hopper Celebration, where I met fellow female students in CS, EE, and other STEM subjects as well as professionals in the field. Though I spent much of my time recruiting at the Career Fair (which was huge and super cool), I was able to attend a few workshops and listen to many inspirational speakers, including Priscilla Chan. Her talk reignited and reaffirmed my passion for social good, reminding me that we as engineers can and will make an impact on the world.

There were also countless after-parties and dinners hosted by various companies each day. I had a great time chatting with Salesforce, Amazon, and GoDaddy employees at their respective parties.

Thank you so much to the UC Berkeley EECS Department for sponsoring me! I’ll definitely be back, whether as a student or full-time engineer.

Europe

July 3-19, 2018

This was a somewhat spontaneous trip, but I honestly had the time of my life.

I bought my plane ticket and, 2 weeks later, flew out to Tivat, Montenegro to join my friend and her family (I know it sounds like I crashed a family vacation, but they actually invited me and convinced me to go!). We spent the week at a beach house, going to the beach during the day and walking around the town at night. I got to meet her friends, cousins, and uncle, and I picked up a bit of Serbian along the way too!

Then, eight of us packed up our bags, squished into a mini-van, and headed to Italy. We stopped at Croatia on the day that the Croatian national team won in the semifinals of the FIFA World Cup. Fireworks surrounded us as we celebrated with the happy locals that night.

In Italy, we stayed in Airbnbs and visited Venice, Florence, San Gimignano, and Rome. From walking 11 miles in Venice to enjoying the nightlife in Rome, and eating tons of gelato, pizza, and pasta throughout, I had a great time! The week simply flew by.

This was also the first time I travelled without my family. It was such a different experience—being part of a European family, I was less like a tourist and felt more immersed in the culture and lifestyle.

Costa Rica

December 27, 2017- January 4, 2018

I rang in 2018 in Costa Rica with my mom, dad, and sister. This was our first family vacation since 2016, and definitely my favorite thus far.

Our week was filled with activities like hiking, whitewater rafting down the Pacuare River, relaxing at beaches, touring Tortuguero National Park, and visiting the town. We met friendly locals, such as our taxi driver Eduardo and rafting guide Alex, who told us all about their beautiful homeland (did you know that Costa Rica has no permanent standing army??). Over the 9 days, I fell in love with this Central American country.
