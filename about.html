About

Hello!

My name is Joy Dong, and I’m a second year at UC Berkeley pursuing a Computer Science major. Despite living in Sunnyvale, CA and growing up with companies like Apple, Google, and Facebook in my backyard, I didn’t take a single coding class in high school because the competitive culture of computer science intimidated me. Looking back, letting my own self-fear dictate my decision to avoid CS was my biggest regret; now, I approach all new things with an open mind, self-confidence, and willingness to learn!

In my free time, I like to go for runs, hike, try new foods, cook, go on adventures, and think about which dog breed I should get when my parents let me finally get a dog, or when I move out and can get one on my own (whichever comes first).

On campus, I’m involved in UC Berkeley ANova, a student organization dedicated to improving computer science education in under-resourced middle and high schools in the area. I'm passionate about social good; through ANova, I can serve the community by teaching computer science, leveling the playing field, and making the CS field accessible to everyone who hopes to pursue it.

Some fun facts: I love stickers, religiously watch the World Cup every four years, and I’m scuba certified.
